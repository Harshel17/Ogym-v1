I am applying to Y Combinator with the following startup. Please evaluate it as if you were a YC partner.

Company: OGym
Category: Fitness / Vertical SaaS

What OGym does:
OGym is a multi-tenant software platform for gyms that gives owners, trainers, and members their own dashboards in one system. Gym owners use OGym to manage memberships, payments, attendance, and analytics. Trainers use it to create and track workout plans and client progress. Members use it to track workouts, attendance, and fitness progress.

Most gyms today use spreadsheets, WhatsApp, billing tools, and separate fitness apps. Members and trainers are forced to use 2–3 disconnected apps, which creates chaos, confusion, and poor retention. OGym replaces all of this with one integrated operating system for the gym.

Traction:
We currently have one gym in an active pilot, with multiple trainers and members using the system daily for workouts, attendance, and management. This is a free pilot while we validate the product. A second gym is in conversation to start another pilot.

Business model:
We charge gym owners a monthly SaaS subscription based on number of members and features. Trainers and members use OGym for free as part of the gym’s plan. Pricing is expected to be $50–$300 per gym per month.

Founder story:
I started OGym because I personally struggled to stay consistent with my workouts. I was using ChatGPT and consumer fitness apps, but it was hard to track what I had done, what I should do next, and how it connected to my gym and trainer. Most fitness apps were confusing, filled with ads, or charged high subscription fees while still not integrating with gyms.

When I spoke with gym owners and trainers, I realized they had the same problem on the business side — they rely on spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and disconnected tools to manage members, workouts, and payments. I built OGym and now have live gyms using it, which validates that this problem is real.

Long-term vision:
OGym becomes the system of record for gym activity (workouts, attendance, payments, member behavior). Once this data exists, we can layer AI on top to predict churn, help trainers generate better programs, and personalize the member experience — without replacing gym staff.

Tech stack:
React + TypeScript frontend, Node.js + Express backend, PostgreSQL database, hosted on Render. Built using Replit Agent with Claude AI for coding. Planning to use Capacitor for mobile apps.
