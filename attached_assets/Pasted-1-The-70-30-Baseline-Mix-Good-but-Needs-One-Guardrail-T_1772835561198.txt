1️⃣ The 70/30 Baseline Mix — Good but Needs One Guardrail

The idea is correct:

70% global standard
30% personal baseline

But without a guardrail, this can create score inflation for beginners.

Example problem:

User baseline = 2k steps

If they walk 4k, the baseline part might give near-100, even though global fitness standards say 4k is still low activity.

Fix

Add a cap on baseline influence.

Recommended rule:

Baseline component cannot exceed the global component by more than +15 points

Example:

Global score = 60
Baseline score = 95

Final pillar score =
(60 × 0.7) + (75 × 0.3)

This keeps improvement rewarded without breaking realism.

2️⃣ Nutrition Baseline Should Use Compliance, Not Calories

Your current idea:

baseline = calorie compliance %

That’s correct — but the key metric should be consistency, not exact calories.

Better baseline signal:

% days meeting calorie range
% days hitting protein target

Because calorie numbers vary daily.

This avoids weird scoring like:

one high-calorie day dropping the score too much
